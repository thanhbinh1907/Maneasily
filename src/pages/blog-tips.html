Mastering Your Workflow with Smart Lists

By Sarah Lee | November 8, 2025

Your to-do list is full. You have tasks spread across ten different projects, and you're not sure what to work on next. This is where Maneasily's **Smart Lists** (using Filters & Labels) become your superpower.

Instead of just viewing tasks by project, Smart Lists let you create custom, dynamic views of your tasks from *across your entire account*.

1. The Building Blocks: Labels & Priorities

Before you build, you need materials. In Maneasily, these are your labels and priorities.

  • Labels: Use these for context. Examples: `@work`, `@home`, `@email`, `@waiting`.
  • Priorities: Use P1 (Urgent), P2 (High), P3 (Medium), P4 (Low) to define importance.

A task can be "Write report" (P1, @work) or "Buy milk" (P2, @home).

2. Creating Your First Smart List

Let's build the most powerful list: "Today's High Priority". This list should show you only what's due today *and* is high priority.

Go to the "Filters & Labels" section in Maneasily and create a new filter with these rules:

Filter Name: My Today
Query: (Due today OR Overdue) & (P1 OR P2)

Now, instead of looking at 10 projects, you have one list that tells you *exactly* what to focus on.

3. Three Smart Lists Everyone Should Have

Here are three templates to get you started:

A. "My Week"

Shows everything you need to do in the next 7 days, grouped by project.

Query: Due in next 7 days

B. "Waiting For"

Shows all tasks you've delegated and are waiting for someone else to complete.

Query: @waiting

C. "Work Focus"

Shows all high-priority tasks in your "Work" project, excluding anything tagged `@email` (for when you want to do deep work).

Query: #Work & (P1 OR P2) & !@email

Start with these, and soon you'll be building custom workflows that fit your life perfectly. That's the power of Smart Lists!
